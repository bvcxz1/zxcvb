Astrometric Microlensing with GAIA*

If dark matter is made up of primordial black holes (PBHs) then these black holes should be scattered all over our galaxy. As stars pass behind these PBHs, their light would get bent around the PBH due to an effect known as gravitational lensing. Modern space telescopes like GAIA can detect tiny glitches in the apparent trajectories of these stars due to this lensing effect. The goal of this project is to explore the sensitivity of GAIA to different masses of PBHs.

I am currently working with Professor Vikram Rentala from the Department of Physics, IIT Bombay to implement an end-to-end pipeline in Python to estimate the blips in the time-series light data from GAIA for point-like and extended lens targets to detect subhalos of dark matter and predict if they are equivalent to PBHs.

  • General Theory of Relativity, Gravitational Lensing
  • Computational Physics, Astrometry, Photometry, Astronomy
  • Primordial Black Holes, Dark Matter, Gamma Ray Bursts

Canopy Height Estimation

Data Sciences for Farming Support Systems for Sustainable Crop Production Under Climate Change project (since September 2017) that involves multi-disciplinary consortium of research institutes under DST-JST scheme of Strategic International Collaborative Research Program (SICORP). The project involves data-science based approaches for high-end integration of information and agricultural sciences such as IoT, Bigdata analytics, crop modelling and omics (genomics and phenomics) to support high performance and sustainable agri-systems in semi-arid tropics of India.

My goal is to implement an algorithm to estimate the height of agricultural crops from drone-based optical images and this is achieved through the generation of dense point clouds. The process started with a literature survey on the topic of Computer Vision and understanding the techniques used in Digital Photogrammetry ranging from Structure from Motion and Multi-View Stereo Algorithms to Poisson 3D Surface Reconstruction Methods. I work under Professor J. Adinarayana, CSRE, IIT Bombay

  • Digital Photogrammetry, Image Processing, Computational Geometry, Data Analysis
  • Structure from Motion, Stereo Correspondance, Depth-Map Estimation, Dense Point Clouds,
    Poisson Surface Reconstruction, Feature Extraction
  • Precision Agriculture, Indian Agricultural Sector

Light-based Feedback Control System

Designed and fully assembled a ‘prototype gravity-wave-aka-light-disturbance-detector’ which included a proportional–integral–derivative controller, actuator and a phototransistor-based plant on a breadboard. It was used to measure the amplitude of a light-based disturbance introduced in the system to great precision.

Conceptually, the above scheme is similar to what the LIGO experiment uses for the detection of gravitational waves. In LIGO, the plant + sensor consists of a Michelson-Morley type light interferometer with the sensor focused on a dark fringe. The feedback controller is tuned precisely to reject ground based noise injected into the interferometer mirrors, i.e, the error in the feedback look is 0 in the steady state. Hence, any extra disturbance detected in this loop is inferred to be from the passage of gravitational waves which move the mirror mass distorting the interferometer arm lengths

  • Operational Amplifiers, PID Controllers, Sensor Characterisation and Calibration

Analysis of Data from Underlying Event Characteristics

Analysed 5.5 Gigabytes of data generated from over 2 million underlying event characteristics from charged particles in proton − proton collisions, using a proprietary particle physics data analysis tool called ROOT. Particles, which are observed as tracks in the detector, were classified based on their rapidity against the direction defined by the leading track, i.e., the particle with the highest momentum. This was performed for all the different possible number of particles emitted in each event, a value which is given by its multiplicity class.

  • ROOT, Data Analysis and Interpretation, High Energy Physics
